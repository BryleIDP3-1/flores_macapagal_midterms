Early Bird vs Night Owl

Greetings! Here we will be discussing the two most common routines that people have in the world regarding their lifestyle. They either love waking up in the morning to a beautiful sunshine and the relaxing sounds of nature or they either prefer the vibes and quietness of the night sky.

They are known to be called as the early birds and the night owls, two types of sleeping phases, patterns, or even personalities. They are famously called this, as every morning when all of nature’s animals such as birds and squirrels wake up early to start the day, most nocturnal animals such as owls, racoons, and bats are wide awake, in sight of the moonlight.

In short terms, other people like to wake up super early in the morning to start their day and get on with their work, while others usually wake up very late and are most productive at night until midnight. This is because either their brain functions are more active at certain times or they have different body clocks and sleeping patterns. Below is where we will discuss what these are, their uniqueness, and which is bestter regarding productivity, health, and other factors.




What is an early bird?

An early bird is usually a person who wakes up very early in the morning to start their day or does things or tasks before other people. They are also known to be “morning persons” or larks. Most of the population are early birds as the standard for work and school are during mornings such as starting at 8am onwards.

Common Traits of an Early Bird

  • They go to bed early (6pm - 10pm)
  • They wake up early (5am - 7am)
  • Feel their best as the day begins in the morning
  • More productive in the early hours of the day
  • Have less energy in the afternoon until the evening
  • Turns sleepy after a certain hour usually at night



Benefits of being an early bird? Why it’s better to be an early bird!

  • Better Quality of Sleep: Sleeping and waking up early can regulate your circadian rhythm, leading to deeper sleeping. This means you feel less tired and more energized.
  • Smoother Work Day: Getting up early means there are less people around in the morning, therefore having faster commutes, short lines at the coffee shop, and for some, less people in popular tourist attractions and destinations.
  • Improve Productivity: As you go early to work or school, you can accomplish more tasks, prevent distractions such as unnecessary breaks, and do more tasks overall.
  • Alignment with the Schedule: The standard time for corporate and school start is early in the morning, therefore you are in-line with the most common time frame of the world.
  • Overall Health: Waking up early, eating a good breakfast, having the time to workout outdoors and be exposed to sunlight can increase your health levels and vitamin D.
  • Increase Happiness: Studies show that waking up in the morning is a great way to boost your happiness, improve mental health and moods, and become more satisfied.



Disadvantages of being a night owl…

  • Health Risks: Staying up late can lead to irregular sleep patterns, which may increase the risk of health issues like obesity, heart disease, & diabetes.
  • Work Schedule Challenges: Traditional 9-to-5 work schedules can be challenging for night owls, leading to sleep deprivation and reduced productivity.
  • Social Isolation: Night owls may have fewer opportunities to socialize with friends and family, as many social activities occur during the day.
  • Less Exposure to Natural Light: Spending more time awake during the night can result in reduced exposure to natural light, which is important for regulating the body's internal clock.
  • Difficulty Adapting: Night owls may struggle to adapt to morning-oriented commitments, such as school or early work meetings, which can lead to chronic fatigue.




What is a night owl?

A night owl is someone who stays up late at night and feels most awake and active during those nighttime hours. They prefer working, socializing, or just being productive during the night, rather than during the daytime. Night owls have a natural tendency to go to bed late and wake up later in the morning.

Common Traits of a Night Owl

  • Stays up late usually until midnight
  • Enjoys sleeping in for long hours
  • Feels their best later in the day
  • Have more energy at night time
  • Feel tired when waking up early
  • Harder times staying alert at day time



Benefits of being a night owl? / Why it’s better to be a night owl!

  • Productivity: Night owls often find it easier to concentrate and work during quiet night time hours when there are fewer distractions.
  • Creativity: Some people feel more creative and inspired during the night, leading to artistic and innovative ideas.
  • Flexibility: Night owls may adapt well to jobs with late-night shifts or remote work, which allows for a more flexible schedule.
  • Peaceful Time: Late nights can offer a peaceful atmosphere for reading, studying, or personal reflection.
  • Social Opportunities: Night owls can enjoy more active social lives as many social events occur in the evenings.
  • Less Crowded: Stores and gyms are often less crowded during nighttime, making it more convenient for night owls to run errands or work out.



Disadvantages of being an early bird…

  • Different Sleeping Patterns: Maybe you might be a night owl and are less productive during the day or you are used to working at night via a shift or a time zone change.
  • Causing Sleep Deprivation: Sleeping and waking up early can lead to fewer hours of sleep, especially if you miss the usual time you sleep. For example, going to bed late and waking up extremely early which can result in less than 8 hours of sleep.
  • Raising Stress Levels: For other people, waking up early can lead to stress and anxiety as sometimes sleep cycles are disrupted and deregulated.
  • Unhealthy habits: Waking up early from a lack of sleep can increase your desire for too much coffee in the morning, which can boost your energy, but can have bad effects.
  • Mood and Concentration: If you are always tired from insufficient hours of sleep, your mood can change, becoming temperamental and also obtain poor concentration.
  • Less Time in Evening: As mentioned, usually early birds work in the morning leading to less time for themselves, activities, and family because they are tired in the evening.



Conclusion

Whether you're a night owl or an early bird, each lifestyle has its own unique advantages and disadvantages. Night owls tend to thrive during the late hours, finding productivity and creativity in the quiet of the night, while early birds are at their best during the daytime, making the most of the morning hours. It's important to recognize your own natural rhythm and find a balance that suits your lifestyle and responsibilities. Ultimately, the key is to maintain a consistent sleep schedule and prioritize good sleep habits to ensure overall well-being, regardless of your preference for staying up late or rising early.
